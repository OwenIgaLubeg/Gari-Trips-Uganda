Frequently Asked Questions

  • 1. How do I obtain a chimpanzee tracking permit?

    Chimpanzee tracking permits can be purchased through the Uganda Wildlife Authority or authorized tour operators. It is advisable to book in advance, as permits are limited and can sell out quickly.

  • 2. Are there any cultural experiences available near Kibale National Park?

    Yes, visitors can engage in cultural experiences with local communities, including visits to traditional villages, learning about local customs, and participating in community projects and activities.

  • 3. Can I see other primates besides chimpanzees in Kibale National Park?

    Yes, Kibale is home to a variety of primate species, including olive baboons, grey-cheeked mangabeys, and several species of monkeys, making it a great destination for primate lovers.

  • 4. Do I have to buy a ticket for my infant?

    Yes, infants typically require a ticket, but it's best to check with the specific tour operator for their policy.

  • 5. What should I pack for a visit to Kibale National Park?
